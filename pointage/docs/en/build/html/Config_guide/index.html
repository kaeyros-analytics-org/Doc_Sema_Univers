Introduction

This guide describes step by step the complete setup of the attendance tracking solution within your company. Let us assume your organisation has two sites, one in Yaoundé and one in Douala, with teams organised into distinct departments and potentially different working hours depending on the site. Here are the steps to follow to correctly configure the tool.

💡
Recommended order

For optimal configuration, follow the steps below in order. Some actions depend on elements created previously: shifts must exist before creating schedules, and employees must be registered before being assigned to a schedule.

1
Step 1

Login to the dedicated platform

As an administrator, log in to the platform using the credentials (email address and password) provided to you at the time of subscription:

🔗 https://pointing-front.kaeyros.org

Once logged in, you are automatically redirected to the attendance logs. Navigation between different sections is done via the left sidebar menu.

2
Step 2

Basic settings configuration

Go to the Settings tab to configure the fundamental elements of your organisation. This step involves four sub-steps to be completed in the following order.

2a — Create departments

Go to the Departments sub-tab (also accessible from the sidebar). Click "Add a department" and enter the name of each department in your company (e.g. Accounting, Production, Logistics). Repeat for each department to create.

2b — Create positions

In the Positions sub-tab, click "Add a position" and enter the name and, if necessary, a description for each job title (e.g. Manager, Technician, Security Officer). These positions will be available when creating employee profiles.

2c — Create sites and define their hours

Go to the Sites & QR Codes tab from the sidebar. Click "Add a site" and follow the two steps of the form:

  • Step 1 — General information: enter the site name (e.g. Yaoundé HQ), its address, time zone and geographic coordinates. Click "Get location" to retrieve them automatically;
  • Step 2 — Working days and hours: select the working days and set the opening and closing hours. You can apply the same hours to all days or customise them day by day.

Click "Confirm". A unique QR code is automatically generated for this site. Repeat for each of your sites (Yaoundé, Douala, etc.).

2d — Create shifts

In the Shifts sub-tab (Settings tab), click "Create a new shift". Enter the shift name, its start and end times, enable the "Night shift" option if necessary, and specify the maximum number of employees that can be assigned. Create as many shifts as needed (e.g. Morning shift 7am–3pm, Night shift 8pm–5am).

💡
Good to know

The shifts created here will be available when building work schedules in step 5. Make sure to create all the shifts you need before moving on.

3
Step 3

Adding additional users

If other people need access to the platform to monitor attendance, add them from the Users sub-tab (Settings tab). Click "Add a user" and enter their name, email address, a password and the company they belong to.

Three roles are available:

Administrator

Full access

Same access as yours — all platform features are available.

HR

Restricted access

Limited access to HR tracking features.

Account Manager

Multi-company

Reserved for resellers — allows managing multiple client companies from a single interface.

Share the email address and password with each user so they can log in to the platform.

4
Step 4

Employee registration

Go to the Employees tab and click "Add an employee". Two addition modes are available:

  • Single addition: manually enter the employee's information — first name, last name, email, phone number, department, position, assigned sites and profile photo (optional);
  • Bulk addition: import multiple employees simultaneously via an Excel or CSV file (.xlsx, .xls, .csv — max 10 MB), selecting the common site, department and position beforehand.
⚠️
Warning — Phone number

Enter the phone number exactly as registered on WhatsApp, with or without the 6 depending on the format (e.g. 23789898989 or 237689898989). An incorrect number will prevent the employee from checking in via WhatsApp.

5
Step 5

Creating work schedules

Go to the Schedules tab and click "Create new schedule". Schedules allow you to associate employees with shifts on defined working days. Fill in the following information:

  • Schedule name: give an explicit name (e.g. Day Schedule Yaoundé, Night Schedule Douala);
  • Working days: select the days to which the schedule applies;
  • Capacity: indicate the maximum number of employees that can be enrolled across all shifts;
  • Shifts and employees: for each shift, select the shift and add the employees to assign. Click "+ Add a shift" to create multiple blocks if needed. An employee can be enrolled in multiple schedules as long as their shifts do not overlap;
  • Dates: choose between Unlimited, a date range or an explicit date list depending on the planned duration of the schedule;
  • Exception dates: enter days to exclude from the validity period (public holidays, exceptional closures, etc.).

Click "Save" to confirm. Repeat for each schedule needed depending on your sites and teams.

💡
Good to know

The system uses schedules to automatically calculate expected attendance, detect absences and measure late arrivals. An employee not assigned to a schedule will not be tracked by the system.

6
Step 6

On-site setup and testing phase

Once configuration is complete, all that remains is to physically deploy the system at your sites.

  • Display the QR code of each site at the entrance (reception or security post). The QR code can be downloaded from the Sites & QR Codes tab by clicking the three dots of the site and then "Download QR". Test it before printing to verify it correctly redirects to the configured WhatsApp number;
  • Inform and train employees on how the system works: scan the QR Code at arrival and at departure, using the WhatsApp number registered on the platform;
  • Plan a test period of at least one week to train teams, correct any phone number errors and validate proper operation of the system across all sites.
💡
Good to know

In case of a missed check-in during the testing or production phase, a manager can manually record an employee's attendance from the Attendance Logs tab by clicking "Record attendance". Absences with no check-in are automatically detected by the system at the end of each scheduled working day.
